One Pot Shrimp Orzo

Description

This is weeknight dinner gold. I love a meal that can be thrown together in a few minutes and tastes like absolute heaven. When I make this, we cannot stop eating it. Every bite is more incredible than the last.

Why is orzo just oh-so good?! Sure, it's like rice, but even better.

I love how it sticks to whatever you toss it with, in a good way. It makes a dish more satisfying too. Plus, it soaks up all the wonderful flavors in the pot.

Ingredients

Shrimp & Marinade
  • 2 lb raw, large, peeled, deveined, & tail-off shrimp (frozen is fine - thaw as below)
  • 6 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼–½ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼–½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
Orzo Base
  • 2 tbsp unsalted vegan butter
  • 16 oz (≈2 ⅔ cups) dry orzo
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced (for toasting)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cups chicken stock (plus extra on hand)
  • 1 tsp granulated chicken bouillon
Veggies & Finish
  • 24 oz frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 - 2 tbsp melted vegan butter for finishing (Optional)

Directions

  1. Thaw & Prep Shrimp
    • Place frozen shrimp in a bowl, cover with cold water, and let sit 15-20 minutes (swap water once halfway).
    • Drain and pat very dry with paper towels.
  2. Marinate Shrimp
    • In a bowl, toss shrimp with olive oil, minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, red pepper flakes, salt & pepper.
    • Let sit 10 minutes while you prep the other ingredients.
  3. Sauté Onions
    • In a large (12″) skillet over medium heat, melt the vegan butter.
    • Add diced onion and cook, stirring often, until translucent (3-4 minutes).
  4. Toast the Orzo
    • Add the dry orzo to the softened onions.
    • Toast, stirring, until the pasta is lightly golden (3-5 minutes).
  5. Sauté Garlic & Peppers
    • Stir in minced garlic and the diced red & green bell peppers.
    • Cook 1-2 minutes until fragrant and peppers begin to soften.
  6. Simmer the Orzo
    • Pour in chicken stock and bring to a boil.
    • Reduce heat, cover, and simmer 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until orzo is tender and most liquid is absorbed.
    • Stir in the granulated bouillon; add extra stock if needed for creaminess.
  7. Add Shrimp & Veggies
    • Nestle the marinated shrimp (and any juices) into the orzo.
    • Stir in the frozen mixed vegetables.
    • Cover and cook 5-7 minutes, until shrimp are opaque and veggies are heated through.
  8. Finish & Serve
    • Remove from heat and, if desired, drizzle melted vegan butter on top.
    • Serve immediately and enjoy!

Notes

  1. If cooking in a pot, spray generously with non-stick cooking spray before heating to prevent sticking.
